01 — the problem · why reinforcement learning
AlphaFold knows the answer.
It cannot explain the path.

AlphaFold2 achieved a scientific breakthrough — predicting the final 3D structure of a protein from its amino acid sequence with near-experimental accuracy. It won the Nobel Prize in 2024.

But AlphaFold is completely silent about the folding pathway — the sequence of physical conformational changes a protein undergoes as it folds. And the pathway is where disease lives.

Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, Type 2 Diabetes — all caused by proteins that misfolded during the journey, not at the destination. No dataset of correct folding pathways exists. Physics is the only teacher.

Reinforcement learning is the only framework honest enough to learn from a physics-based reward signal without requiring labelled examples of correct folding trajectories.

01
No labelled data exists
Supervised learning requires (input, correct output) pairs. No database of correct folding pathways exists at atomic resolution. RL learns without labels — reward is physics.
02
Sequential decisions
Folding is a sequential process — each dihedral angle adjustment depends on the current conformation. RL naturally models sequential decision-making over time.
03
Physics as reward
The Lennard-Jones energy function provides a differentiable, physics-grounded reward signal. Lower energy = more stable structure = better fold.
04
Exploration of pathways
The PPO entropy bonus encourages exploration of novel folding pathways — not just greedy energy minimisation. This mirrors how proteins actually explore conformational space.

03 — model architecture · graph neural network
GNN forward pass
Graph Neural Network Policy

The protein is represented as a molecular graph where each residue is a node and spatial contacts within 8 Angstroms are edges. This preserves the physical topology of the molecule — unlike flat feature vectors.

Message passing allows each residue to aggregate information from its spatial neighbours over 4 rounds — capturing both local secondary structure and global fold topology simultaneously.

The global mean pool compresses the entire protein into a single 256-dimensional vector — the "state embedding" used by both the policy and value heads.

Node input 20-dim AA one-hot + 3-dim Cα coords = [N, 23]
Edge input distance + direction + peptide flag = [E, 4]
Hidden dim 128 node · 64 edge · 256 global
Layers 4× MPNN with edge-conditioned messages
Activation GELU throughout
Init Orthogonal weights · gain=0.01
Policy out logits [1, N×2×12] → Categorical dist
Value out scalar V(s) for GAE advantage estimation
04 — energy function · physics-based reward signal
The Physics Teacher

The reward signal comes entirely from a coarse-grained physics potential — no human labels, no expert demonstrations. This is justified by its use in MARTINI coarse-grained models, published in Nature Methods.

Lennard-Jones potential (pairwise)
E_LJ = 4ε [ (σ/r)¹² − (σ/r)⁶ ]
r = Cα–Cα distance · ε = well depth · σ = collision diameter
Repulsion dominates at short range · attraction at medium range
Torsion energy (Ramachandran)
E_tor = Σ k[1 + cos(nφ − δ)]
φ, ψ = backbone dihedral angles · n = periodicity · δ = phase
Penalises sterically forbidden Ramachandran regions
Total energy
E_total = E_LJ + E_torsion
Computed at every step · delta used as reward signal · lower = more stable = better fold

05 — PPO-clip · proximal policy optimisation
Why PPO-clip?

PPO-clip prevents catastrophic policy updates by clipping the probability ratio between the old and new policy. This ensures stable training even with a high-dimensional, physics-driven reward signal.

PPO-clip objective
L = E[ min(r·A, clip(r, 1−ε, 1+ε)·A) ]
r = π_new / π_old · A = GAE advantage · ε = 0.2
Clip prevents destructively large policy updates
GAE advantage (λ=0.95)
A_t = Σ (γλ)^k δ_{t+k}
δ = TD error · γ = 0.99 discount · λ = 0.95 bias-variance tradeoff
Lower variance than Monte Carlo · lower bias than TD(0)

06 — scientific justification · judge questions answered
Q1
Why simplified energy, not full MD?
Full molecular dynamics takes hours per protein on a supercomputer — incompatible with a real-time RL training loop that runs thousands of steps per second.

Our Lennard-Jones backbone potential captures the dominant forces governing secondary structure formation. This is the same approximation used in MARTINI coarse-grained models — published in Nature Methods and used by research groups globally. Scientifically justified. Computationally necessary.
Q2
Can a hackathon project contribute anything real?
We are not claiming to cure Alzheimer's in 36 hours. We are demonstrating that RL with a physics-based reward is a viable framework for exploring folding pathways — never demonstrated in an open, accessible way before.

AlphaFold itself began as a proof of concept at a CASP competition. Every major breakthrough starts as a demonstration the approach works. This is ours.
Q3
Why GNN and not a simpler model?
Proteins are graphs — residues connected by covalent bonds and spatial contacts. A flat MLP treats the protein as an unordered feature vector and loses all structural information.

GNNs are the natural architecture for graph-structured data. Message passing propagates local chemical information across the full protein topology — exactly what is needed to make spatially-aware folding decisions.
